The Coriolis Force

An extra term appears in the momentum equation going as Dvdt=(other terms)+Ω×v. We may account for how this changes the mode frequencies by

  • Using perturbation theory (discussed later), or
  • Modifying the pulsation boundary value problem.

The Traditional Approximation for Rotation

We replace Ω=Ωez→(Ω⋅er)er=Ωcos⁡θer. This causes the pulsation problem to remain amenable to separation of variables.

Recall: this means solutions are of the general form, e.g.,
P(r,θ,φ)=P~(r)Θ(θ)Φ(φ).

By azimuthal symmetry, Φ(φ)=eimφ for integer m.

Laplace’s Tidal Equation

Without rotation, the solution in the latitude coordinate, Θ, satisfies ddμ(1−μ2ddμΘ)−m2(1−μ2)Θ=λΘ.

This is Laplace’s equation with eigenvalues λ=ℓ(ℓ+1) for integers ℓ.

With rotation, this becomes Laplace’s tidal equation: [ddμ(1−μ21−q2μ2ddμ)−m2(1−μ2)(1−q2μ2)+mq(1+q2μ2)(1−q2μ2)2]Θ=λΘ, with the spin parameter q=2Ω/ω.

Solutions are given by the Associated Legendre Polynomials, Pℓm(cos⁡θ).

Solutions are given by the
Hough Functions, Hℓm(cos⁡θ,q).
